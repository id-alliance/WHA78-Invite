Health Without Borders – Integrated Primary Healthcare for All

(Organized by IDA, Sightsavers, ASTMH, GHC, PATH, IDDC, YHO and Amref Health Africa)
 
 

With shifting donor priorities and growing funding uncertainties, essential health programs—including those tackling infectious diseases, NCDs, maternal and child health, and health system strengthening—face increasing challenges. Now more than ever, sustainable financing mechanisms are needed to ensure long-term investments in global health, including those that spur the development of new tools and strengthen the research ecosystem, saving lives, and bringing wider economic benefits.

 

Objectives:


🔹 Discuss the changing global health financing landscape and risks posed by declining donor funding.
 🔹 Explore innovative financing models (blended finance, public-private partnerships, catalytic funding).
 🔹 IDA is convening key stakeholders to advance the outcomes of this discussion through the Global Health Financing Coalition—a platform dedicated to driving sustainable investments in global health. If you're interested in joining, please reach out.

📅 May 21, 2025
🕙10:20 – 12:00 CEST (with access to the room 20 minutes before start)

📍Civil Society Networking Space, Salle Nyon, 9–11 Rue de Varembé, 1211 Geneva 20
Register Here
 
 

This session will emphasize the importance of integrated primary healthcare (IPHC) in tackling key global health challenges such as HIV, TB, diabetes, and hypertension. The discussion will highlight policy integration, improved diagnosis and care frameworks, and cross-sector collaboration to strengthen universal health coverage (UHC).

 

Objectives:


🔹 Promote policy and advocacy for cost-effective and sustainable IPHC solutions.
🔹 Strengthen health equity and inclusion, ensuring access for marginalized populations.
🔹 Foster multisectoral collaboration among governments, international agencies, donors, and civil society. 

🔹 IDA is bringing together key stakeholders to establish a lasting impact by convening key stakeholders in the Primary Healthcare Coalition—an ongoing platform for dialogue, policy coordination, and advocacy.
